L

The Holy Spirit

The Indwelling OneSource of Power

Core Truth

The Holy Spirit, the Third Person of the Trinity, took up His abode in the world at Pentecost. He dwells in every believer, unites all to Christ in one body, and is the source of all power and acceptable worship and service. He never departs from the church nor from the feeblest saint.

His Indwelling Presence

John 14:16-17

Another Helper (allosanother of the same kind) who abides forever

1 Corinthians 6:19

Your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit

Ephesians 2:22

Being built together for a dwelling place of God in the Spirit

Romans 8:9

If anyone does not have the Spirit of Christ, he is not His

His Ministries

John 16:7-11

Convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment

1 Corinthians 12:13

Baptizes all believers into one body

Ephesians 4:30

Seals us unto the day of redemption

Ephesians 5:18

Be filled with the Spiritcontinued filling for power

2 Thessalonians 2:7

Restrains evil in the world

1 John 2:20-27

Anoints and teaches believers

Indwelling vs. Filling

Every believer has the indwelling of the Spirit from salvation (1 Cor. 6:19). The filling is for power, teaching, and serviceand can be lost through disobedience. We are commanded to be filled (Eph. 5:18) and not to quench (1 Thess. 5:19) or grieve (Eph. 4:30) the Spirit.

M

The Church

A Unity of BelieversBody & Bride of Christ

Core Truth

All who are united to the risen and ascended Son of God are members of the church, which is the body and bride of Christ. It began at Pentecost and is completely distinct from Israel. By the same Spirit, all believerswhether Jews or Gentilesare baptized into one body.

Distinct from Israel

  • Israel: Old Covenant; Church: New Covenant
  • Israel: No indwelling Spirit; Church: Spirit indwells
  • Church is the "body of Christ"

Foundation

Unity

Keep the unity of the Spirit

Colossians 3:14-15

Bond of perfection; peace of God rule

The Ordinances

Baptism & The Lord's Supper

Core Truth

Water baptism and the Lord's Supper are the only sacraments and ordinances of the church. They are a scriptural means of testimony for the church in this agenot a means of salvation, but an outward expression of an inward reality.

Water Baptism

Public declaration of faithidentification with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection

Crispus and many Corinthians believed and were baptized

O

The Christian Walk

Spirit vs. Flesh

Core Truth

We are called with a holy calling to walk not after the flesh but after the Spirit. The flesh with its fallen, Adamic nature is never eradicated in this life and needs to be kept by the Spirit constantly in subjection to Christ, or it will surely manifest in our lives.

Walking in the Spirit

Romans 6:11-13

Reckon yourselves dead to sin, alive to God; present your members to God

Romans 8:2, 4, 12-13

Law of the Spirit of life; walk according to the Spirit; put to death the deeds of the body

Galatians 5:16-23

Walk in the Spirit and you shall not fulfill the lust of the flesh; fruit of the Spirit

Practical Holiness

Ephesians 4:22-5:2

Put off the old man, put on the new man; walk in love

Colossians 2:6-10

As you received Christ, walk in Him, rooted and built up

1 Peter 1:14-16

As obedient children... be holy, for I am holy

1 John 1:4-7, 3:5-9

Walk in the light; whoever abides in Him does not practice sin

P

The Christian's Service

Spiritual Gifts & Rewards

Core Truth

Divine, enabling gifts for service are bestowed by the Spirit upon all who are saved. While there is diversity of gifts, each believer is energized by the same Spirit. Rewards are promised according to faithfulness and will be bestowed at the judgment seat of Christ.

Spiritual Gifts

Romans 12:6

Gifts differing according to the grace givenuse them!

1 Corinthians 12:4-11

Diversity of gifts, same Spirit; different ministries, same Lord

Ephesians 4:11

He gave apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachersfor equipping

1 Timothy 5:17-18; Luke 10:7

The laborer is worthy of his wagespastors should be supported

Rewards at the Judgment Seat

1 Corinthians 3:9-15

Each one's work will be tested by firegold, silver, precious stones vs. wood, hay, stubble

1 Corinthians 9:18-27

Run to win the prize; discipline your body; motivation matters

2 Corinthians 5:10

We must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ to receive for things done

Note:

This is the judgment seat (bema) for believers' rewardsnot the Great White Throne for unbelievers. Salvation is not in question; faithfulness and service are being evaluated.
